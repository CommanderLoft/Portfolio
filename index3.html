Why I love Video Games

One of the biggest things about me is that I LOVE playing video games. Ever since my dad got me into them when I was 4, when he was playing The Legend of Zelda Twilight Princess on the Wii, I have always been a fan of video games. While I could ramble on for hours about new and interesting adventures I've had while experiencing these great nuggets of wonderful media, I've made a list of my top 5 favorite video games below. While my explanation skills aren't the best, I can't stress how good these games are. I would definitely reccommend playing them.

Kid Icarus really sticks out to me because of it's use of humor while telling a compelling story full of fun twists. Not even the game takes itself seriously sometimes! Heck, they even brake the 4th wall. I don't know, it's the lightheartedness that really gives it it's charm, along with a surprising amount of strategy that goes into the game with weapon fusion and the limited amount of spells you can use. Another good thing about the game is the music. Since the game's music and story were all made by Mr. Sakurai himself, he made sure to time the music exactly witht what's going on in the story. Honestly, I don't think I can really do this game justice in a short paragraph, but rather Kid Icarus: Uprising is more of an experience you have to try for yourself.

Link's Awakening is my favorite entry out of the whole The Legend of Zelda series just because of the beautiful story. You wake up on a island called Kohilint, and you discover that the only way off the island is to awaken the Wind Fish with the hauntingly beautiful Ballad of the Wind Fish. However, along your journey, you discover the shocking truth about Kohilint with makes you question everything you were fighting for. And I'll be real. Link's Awakening made me cry at the end. And I don't really cry for video games. But especially with the Switch remake of this charming game, it felt all the more magical seeing Kohilint with a new coat of paint. To me, Link's Awakening is a special feeling that I will forever hold dear in my heart.

Pokemon Super Mystery Dungeon is another one of my favorite games because it's an interesting take on the Pokemon series as a whole. Instead of the traditional RPG style Pokemon is known for, the Pokemon Mystery Dungeon series is instead known for it's brilliant writing, and Super Mystery Dungeon definitely falls into that category. In fact, this is another game that made me cry, and only 3 games have made me cry before. Granted, while this game is more item focused than its predecessors, the experience and story is worth it.

Splatoon 3 is another one of my favorite games because not only am I on the Lane Tech Splatoon 3 esports team, it's just one of those games you can just have fun messing around in. Also in terms of competitive, fast paced games, this is a good example of a game where teamwork is key. In fact, it still is getting new weapon and item updates even today, so the meta is constantly updating. And even if you're not that into competitive fast paced games, the story mode also isn't that bad.

Dragon Quest IX is one of my favorite games because it's one of those games you can really sink your teeth into. No really, the estimated time to 100% it is 711 hours. In terms of story, Dragon Quest IX is in my opinion the best written Dragon Quest game in the series. I just have a lot of fond memories playin it alongside my dad's strategy guide when I was nine years old, and was just in awe of the writing. Heck, I didn't even finish all the things to do in post-game as theres SO MUCH TO DO. It's kinda insane that they managed to fit all of that content on the DS of all consoles. But this is the best RPG of all time in my opinion.

I'm even a part of the Lane Tech Splatoon Esports team, as LoftTheSkylord!

So, where did this debate come from?

Well, this dumb arguement occcured when he sent me this meme right here to the right, with a caption of: "Biggest upgrade in history." However, that got my masochistic brain working and I decided to waste an hour of my life using a Pokemon damage calculator to prove why the "upgrade" wasn't even an upgrade. What exactly was it, you ask? Well, here it is in all its glory.

However, before we begin this pointless arguementatitve essay for a topic no one cares about, I am laying down the one ground rule that while it is possible for the Groudon to have only ground-type moves that can't hit the Rowlet, as flying types are immune to flying type moves, that's not allowed as Groudon doesn;t usually only have ground-type moves. Also, to give Rowlet a good shot of actually being able to win, I'm giving Groudon 0 IV (Individual values) across the board. To sum up IVs quickly, when you catch a Pokemon, they are randomly assigned Individualized values on a scale of 0-31. The higher the IV is, the better stats the Pokemon has. Once a Pokemon has it's alloted IVs, the only way to raise them is to use a Bottle Cap at the Hyper Training person in later games. Otherwise, if you don't use Hyper Training, or you're playing an entry in the series before Gen VII, the IVs a Pokemon has are permamenant. However, to give Rowlet an even better chance at winning this battle, I'm also not going to give Groudon any EVs. EVs, (Effort Values) are not permamenant, unlike IVs, but you can only alot a max of 508 EVs per Pokemon. To completely max out a certain stat, like Speed for example, you need to alot 252 out of your possible 508 EVs into that stat. This is just the simpified version, as there is a lot more explanation on how to breed Pokemon with good IVs and Ev Training, but basically, 31 IVs and 252 EVs means that you completely maxed out that particular stat for a Pokemon. Also, not only will Groudon not have any IVs or EVs, but it will always be level 45, as that's the lowest legal level you can get a Groudon at, while Rowlet's level will be scaled as needed, and Rowlet will have max IVs and max EVs in Attack and Special Attack, to really try and give it a chance.

To start off, I set Groudon to be level 45, as that is the lowest legal level a Groudon can be at, and I set Rowlet to be at level 16, as a Rowlet would've evolved by level 16. As you can expect, even a very debuffed Groudon still OHKOs the Rowlet, and the Rowlet doesn't stand a chance.


Well, if we want to make this hypothetical scenario more accurate to the meme where it originated from, then we'll have to set Rowlet's level to be 70, as you can see it's level in the meme. However, despite the level advantage it has over Groudon, the best it can settle for is a chance at a 2HKO due to it's inferior stats as compared to Groudon's.


Heck, since I REALLY wanted for Rowlet to win this fight at this point, I just made it level 100, but even at level 100, with Dynamax to boost it's stats, max IVs, and all of it's EVs allocated against a Groudon with no EVs and no IVs, it still only has an 81.3% chance to OHKO. It's not even guranteed at the maximum level a Pokemon can reach, and against a level 45 Groudon to boot. I have no words. It's just impossible for Rowlet to get a guranteed OHKO on Groudon.
